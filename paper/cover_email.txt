I am a final-year mathematics undergraduate. I first met these

The observation: applying the univalent arithmetic holonomy bound
(Theorem 2.7.1 of arXiv:2408.15403) to the derivative tower
{1, P, theta P, ..., theta^{k-1} P}, where P = pA - qV is the
conditional rational combination attached to an Apéry pair annihilated
by an irreducible operator of order k >= 2, lowers the threshold of
the determinantal criterion (Constr. Approx. 45 (2017)) from
log(4 x_2) > (3/2) sigma to

    log(4 x_2) > sigma (k+2)/(k+1).

The attached 6-page note states this precisely and proves it modulo
your published theorem. The only inputs about the tuple are:
Q(x)-linear independence (proved from irreducibility in the
left-Euclidean ring), exact denominator types (preserved by theta),
holonomicity with d/dx-stable span (so Shidlovsky's lemma applies with
epsilon = 0), and holomorphy on the slit plane. For Apéry's own
zeta(3) data the classical radius argument of course already applies;
the content lies in the window e^{sigma(k+2)/(k+1)}/4 < x_2 <=
e^sigma, and in the resulting specification table for linear forms in
1 and zeta(5) (Section 4 of the note).

Three questions:

1. Is the application valid, or have we misread a hypothesis of
   Theorem 2.7.1 / 7.0.1?

2. Is the statement already known to you, in folklore or in print?

3. Section 6 reports exact-arithmetic measurements of the denominators
   actually realized by the Siegel constructions for the Apéry system
   (tuples {1, V, theta V} and {1, V, theta V, theta^2 V}): the
   observed rates are about 1.76 and 1.89 against the tau-cap, with
   the savings concentrated at small primes. Is this phenomenon
   documented anywhere?

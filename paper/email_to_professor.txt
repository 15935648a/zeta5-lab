I am a final-year student in your complex analysis course this
semester. When you introduced the Riemann zeta function in class, I
became curious about the irrationality of its odd values (Apery's

    For Apery-style linear forms l_n = u_n*eta - v_n (u_n integers,
    v_n with denominators of type lcm(1..n)^sigma) whose generating
    function sum l_n x^n continues holomorphically to the slit plane
    C \ [x_2, oo), and whose underlying differential operator is
    irreducible of order k over Q(x), the inequality

        log(4 x_2) > sigma (k+2)/(k+1)

    implies eta is irrational.

For comparison, Apery's classical mechanism needs log x_2 > sigma, and
Zudilin's determinantal criterion (Constr. Approx. 45, 2017) needs
log(4 x_2) > (3/2) sigma; the new point is that the order of the
differential equation acts as an arithmetic resource. The proof is a
direct application of a published theorem of Calegari--Dimitrov--Tang
(arXiv:2408.15403, Theorem 2.7.1); our own part is an independence
lemma (one page, left Ore division), the verification that the
derivative tower preserves the arithmetic type, and the numerical
experiments of Section 6.

Some of this may be close to your interests: the sequences are
holonomic, the coefficient asymptotics x_2^{-n} n^{-3/2} come from a
square-root singularity, and Section 6 is an exact-arithmetic
experimental study of the denominators realized by Siegel-type
constructions, where the observed savings concentrate at small primes.
